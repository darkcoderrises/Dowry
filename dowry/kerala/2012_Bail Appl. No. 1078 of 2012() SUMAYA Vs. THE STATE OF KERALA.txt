                     ORDER


    Petitioner is the fourth accused in Crime

No.272/2011  of   Eravipuram   Police  Station

registered  for  the  offences  under  sections

120B, 420, 498A read with section 34 of Indian

Penal Code and section 4 and 6(2) of Dowry

Prohibition Act based on a complaint filed

before the Judicial First Class Magistrate-I,

Kollam and sent for investigation under section

156(3)  of  the  Code  of  Criminal  Procedure.

Apprehending arrest petition is filed under

section 438 of the Code of Criminal Procedure

for anticipatory bail.

    2.   Learned senior counsel appearing for

the   petitioner   and   the   learned   Public

Prosecutor were heard.

      3.    Learned senior counsel pointed out that the

brother of the petitioner married the de facto

complainant     on  15.4.2010  and  the   marriage  was

subsisting till 31.7.2010 and the petitioner was

given in marriage on 25.7.2010 and since then she

has been residing either in matrimonial home or

abroad and petitioner has not committed any offence

and in such circumstance she be released on bail in

the event of her arrest.

      4.    Learned Public Prosecutor submitted that in

the private complaint allegation is made against

the      petitioner    also   that    she   has    also

misappropriated the amount belonging to the sister-

in-law of the petitioner.

      5.    Considering the nature of the offence, I do

not    find    that  custodial  interrogation  of   the

petitioner is necessary. In the event of arrest of

the petitioner in Crime 272/2011 of Eravipuram

Police Station, she      shall be released on bail on

executing a bond for Rs.20,000/- with two solvent

sureties each for the like sum to the satisfaction
