       Petition for anticipatory bail.

       2. The alleged offences are under sections 498A read with

section 34 IPC.     According to prosecution, de facto complainant

married 1st accused and thereafter, petitioners who are father-in-

law and mother-in-law along with 1st accused husband harassed

de facto complainant, demanding more dowry. She was assaulted

by her husband at the instigation of petitioners.

       3.     Learned counsel for petitioners submitted that

petitioners are accused 2 and 3. They are retired government

servants. The allegations made against them are false. There are

only general allegations that they instigated 1st accused to

commit the offence, it is submitted.

       4. Learned Public Prosecutor submitted that the main

allegations are against 1st accused and hence he has no objection

in granting anticipatory bail to petitioners.

       5. Hence, the following order is passed:-

       (1)    Petitioners       shall      surrender        before      the

             Investigating Officer within seven days from

             today and they shall be released on bail, on

             their executing a bond for Rs. 25,000/- each
