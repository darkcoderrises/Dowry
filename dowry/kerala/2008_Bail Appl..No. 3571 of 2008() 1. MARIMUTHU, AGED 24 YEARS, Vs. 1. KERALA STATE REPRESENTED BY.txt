         Application for bail.

         2. The alleged offences are under sections 302 and 304B IPC.

         3. According to the prosecution, the petitioner harassed his wife

making unlawful demand for dowry and she committed suicide on 21-4-

2008 by hanging. She also committed murder of six months' old daughter.

         4. The learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that offence

under section 302 IPC will not be attracted against the petitioner. It is

submitted that on 25-4-2008 the petitioner surrendered before the learned

Magistrate and he was remanded to judicial custody. The petitioner was

interrogated by the police. In the above circumstances, the further detention

of the petitioner may not be required for the purpose of investigation in this

case, it is submitted.

         5. The learned Public Prosecutor submitted that this is an

unfortunate case where the deceased was forced to murder her own child

and committed suicide by hanging because of the cruelty of her husband.

However, the detention of the petitioner may not be required for

investigation, he has no objection in granting bail to the petitioner on

stringent conditions.

        6. Hence, the petitioner is granted bail on the following terms and

conditions: -

        1) The petitioner shall be released on bail on his executing a bond

for Rs.50,000/- with two solvent sureties each for the like amount to the

satisfaction of the trial court.

        2)The petitioner shall report before the Investigating Officer on

alternate days between 10 a.m. and 1 p.m. until further orders.

        3) The petitioner shall not leave the limits of the police station,

within which the crime is registered except with the prior permission of the

learned Magistrate.

        4) The petitioner shall not intimidate or influence any witness while

on bail and in case breach of this condition, bail is liable to be cancelled.

        The application is allowed.
