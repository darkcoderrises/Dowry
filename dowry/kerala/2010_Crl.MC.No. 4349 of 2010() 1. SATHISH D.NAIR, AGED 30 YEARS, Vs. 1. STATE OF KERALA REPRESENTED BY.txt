                          O R D E R


         Petitioners 1 to 4 are the accused

and  fifth      petitioner,                   the       de facto

complainant      in         Crime            No.304/2010     of

Chittoor Police Station,                     registered under

Annexure-A   FIR         for         the        offence    under

Sections 498 A and 386                    read with Section

34 of Indian Penal Code and Section 4 of

Dowry Prohibition Act on the basis of the

complaint filed by the fifth petitioner

before Judicial First Class Magistrate's

Court, Chittoor and sent for investigation

under Section 156(3) of Code of Criminal

Procedure. Petition is filed jointly by the

accused and de facto complainant stating

that the entire matrimonial disputes were

                           2


settled       amicably  and  consequent   to   the

settlement,      it  is  not in  the  interest of

justice to continue the prosecution.

            2.   Learned counsel appearing for the

petitioners and learned Public Prosecutor     were

heard.

            3. As   held  by the  Apex   Court  in

B.S.Joshi      and others  v. State of Haryana and

another (2003(4) SCC 675), when the matrimonial

disputes      were  settled amicably  between  the

husband and the wife, it is not in the interest

of justice to continue the prosecution. Crime

itself was registered      on a complaint filed by

the fifth petitioner wife. Wife along with the

husband and in-laws filed this petition stating

that they have settled all the disputes and

therefore, the case is to be quashed. In such

circumstances, it is not in the interest of

justice to continue the prosecution.

            Petition is allowed. Crime No.304/2010

of Chittoor Police Station is quashed.
